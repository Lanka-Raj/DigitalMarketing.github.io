Mission

"To preserve, promote, and celebrate Bhutanese artistic heritage by showcasing traditional and contemporary crafts, fostering cultural exchange, and supporting local artisans. We aim to provide a vibrant space for appreciating the beauty and richness of Bhutanese creativity, while nurturing a deeper understanding of the nation's unique artistic legacy."

History of Druk Zorig

Origins

The gallery was born out of a deep respect for Bhutanese art and culture. The founders envisioned a venue dedicated to displaying traditional Bhutanese arts and crafts, including Thangka paintings, woodwork, textiles, and metalwork.

Growth and Influence

From its early days, the gallery played a significant role in Bhutan's cultural revival, organizing exhibitions, workshops, and events that attracted visitors from around the world. It has become known for its authentic and intricately crafted Bhutanese art.

Support And Artisans

Druk Arts and Craft Gallery has been actively involved in supporting local artisans. By providing a platform for them to showcase their work, the gallery has contributed to the growth and recognition of Bhutanese traditional arts, offering artisans new opportunities and visibility.

Expanding Horizons

Embracing the digital era, the gallery has extended its reach by launching online platforms, making Bhutanese art accessible to a global audience through its website and social media.

Future Directions

Today, Druk Arts and Craft Gallery remains dedicated to its mission of preserving and promoting Bhutanese cultural heritage. With a commitment to high-quality craftsmanship and a deep passion for the arts, the gallery continues to celebrate and share Bhutan's rich artistic legacy.
